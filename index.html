About me!

I am a graduate student in Analysis and Political Economy: Sustainability, Corporations and Policies at Université Paris Cité, where my thesis sits at the intersection of climate finance and risk management. Using a quasi-experimental design, I examine how the insurance sector's withdrawal from carbon-intensive activities influences corporate environmental behavior and firms' transition strategies.

This work continues a research program that began during my MPhil at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), where I studied corporate sustainability and ESG trajectories using predictive machine learning. The shift from prediction to causal inference in my current thesis reflects what I want to pursue in doctoral research: combining theoretical reflection with rigorous empirical analysis to understand how financial institutions and market constraints shape real corporate decisions.

Research Interests

My research program is organized around a central question — how financial-sector frictions and constraints translate into changes in real corporate behavior, particularly in the context of the climate transition. Within this, I work on:

  • Climate finance and corporate transition — how insurance underwriting, capital availability, and investor pressure affect firms' carbon intensity and environmental strategies.
  • Risk management and financial regulation — regulatory frameworks, ESG risk, and the role of financial institutions in steering capital toward sustainable activities.
  • Empirical methods in finance — quasi-experimental and causal inference designs applied to firm-level data, complemented by machine-learning approaches to ESG trajectories.
  • Sustainability and policy evaluation — corporate sustainability benchmarks, energy transition pathways, and the link between firm behavior and broader policy outcomes.
